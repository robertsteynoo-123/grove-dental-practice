Our Treatments

Dental Care & Services

Select a treatment below to view description and pricing information.

Check-ups & Dental Care Advice

Personalised guidance on brushing, flossing, diet and prevention, tailored to your needs. We'll help you build simple habits that keep your teeth and gums healthy between visits. A great starting point if you want to take better care of your smile.

Private Cost: from £55 NHS Cost: Band 1 — £26.80

Scale & Polish

A professional clean to remove plaque and tartar build-up that standard brushing misses. Includes a gentle polish to remove external stains and leave your teeth feeling fresh and smooth. Essential for maintaining clean gums.

Private Cost: from £75 NHS Cost: Band 1 — £26.80 (when clinically necessary)

Fillings

Restorative treatment to repair structural damage caused by decay or chips. We use advanced materials to pack and seal the affected area, preserving your tooth and protecting it from further infection. High-quality restorative care.

Private Cost: from £120 NHS Cost: Band 2 — £73.50

Root Canal Treatment

Carefully cleaning out infected or inflamed pulp inside a tooth's root system to relieve toothache and prevent extraction. Once disinfected, the canal is sealed and filled. A highly effective way to save a severely damaged tooth.

Private Cost: from £450 NHS Cost: Band 2 — £73.50

Gum Disease Treatment

Clinical treatments for gingivitis or periodontitis to control infections and stop gum recession. This includes deep scaling, root planing, and personalized hygienic instruction to protect the bone structure supporting your teeth.

Private Cost: from £100 NHS Cost: Band 2 — £73.50

Crowns & Bridges

Premium covers shaped like natural teeth to protect and strengthen broken or weakened teeth (Crowns), or custom dental prosthetics anchored to healthy teeth to bridge the gap left by missing teeth (Bridges).

Private Cost: Crowns from £600 · Bridges from £700 per unit NHS Cost: Band 3 — £319.10

Tooth Extractions

Safe, gentle removal of non-restorable, broken, or infected teeth. We use pain-free local anesthetics and provide detailed aftercare instructions to ensure rapid, comfortable healing and gum recovery.

Private Cost: from £150 NHS Cost: Band 2 — £73.50

NHS treatments follow standard regulatory price bands: Band 1 £26.80 · Band 2 £73.50 · Band 3 £319.10.

All prices and treatments are subject to assessment — please confirm at consultation.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Clear information about treatments, policies, and what to expect.

NHS treatments follow fixed price bands set by the government (Band 1: £26.80, Band 2: £73.50, Band 3: £319.10). Private treatments vary depending on complexity. Check-ups start from £55 privately. We provide a full, transparent breakdown of costs and a written treatment plan before any work begins.

Yes, we are a mixed practice offering both NHS treatments and private dentistry. You can choose to have your core treatments on the NHS and select specific cosmetic options (like white composite fillings or teeth whitening) privately if you wish.

Nervous patients are our specialty. Our dentists (Dr Hussain, Dr Ansari, and Dr Julka) are highly praised for their gentle, unhurried approach. We listen to your concerns, explain treatments step-by-step, let you take breaks, and progress entirely at your own comfort speed.

In a dental emergency (severe pain, bleeding, swelling, or a knocked-out tooth) during opening hours, please call our reception on 020 8552 1953 immediately. We keep dedicated daily slots open for emergencies. Out of hours, please call NHS 111 for advice and emergency support.

At your first visit, we will welcome you, discuss your medical history, and complete a thorough check of your teeth, gums, and mouth. We will take digital X-rays if necessary. If you need further treatment, we will explain your options and print out a clear cost breakdown plan.

Our capacity to take on new NHS and private patients varies throughout the year. Please contact our reception desk on 020 8552 1953 or fill out the appointment request form below, and we will advise you of our current availability.

For most patients, we recommend a routine dental check-up every 6 to 12 months to monitor health and catch issues early. Depending on your dental health, gum conditions, and risk of decay, your dentist might suggest a different interval.

Yes, we welcome patients of all ages, including infants and children. Helping children develop positive associations with the dentist and learning good brushing habits early is essential to setting up a lifetime of healthy smiles.
